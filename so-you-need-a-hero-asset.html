So you need a hero asset

Date Wed 31 July 2019 Tags dev / 3d

Midlothian Hero Ship Development

Proof of concept

When I began developing CapitalSpace I spent a few bucks on the Unity asset store to get some placeholder spaceships and other pieces to use in proof of concepts. I eventually reached a point where I needed to start creating mechanics and not be fiddling with constant scaling issues. I reached out to some contacts and was able to secure a fantastic Sci-Fi artist to start working on the Hero ship and hopefully in the future some UI assets.

What did we need?

That's a good question, and since we haven't finished this process I can't outline everything. However my main goal was to have a solid tangible base, and what better place to start than the thing the player will spend most of their time looking at. With that said there were some basic requirements.

  • Length: ~1km

  • Armaments

    • ~24 Main Turreted Weapon Stations
    • ~12 Main Missile Silos
    • ~100x Point Defense Cannons

    :::python

    NOTE

    There are some notes here first and foremost being that all of this is up in the air aside from the

    length of the ship, and the number of armaments is heavily influenced by the Galactica from the 2004

    reboot.

Knowing the scale of our ship being 1km will help us scale out other objects in the universe.

Hero Asset

Starting Out

The first stage in creating our hero asset is to have a good idea of what you are looking for, but also realize that you are hiring a 3d artist, and they are creating for you whatever image you give them. I know what I like in a Capital ship from an aesthetic view, and having spent some time learning about naval ships over history, I believe I have a pretty good idea of what form would follow the function of a capital ship that needed to have several functions beyond "Seal Clubbing Warship." So I reached out to a few folks to see if they were interested in tackling a hero asset, and potentially more down the road. When I found an artist whos work I enjoyed and I felt comfortable with we sat down to hash out the details.

Pay your fucking artists

You can't buy food or electricity with exposure

It's simple, if you think that you have enough exposure that you can pay your artist in it, you do not. 3D art is a real skill, not everyone is the same, and you and your artist need to have a professional adult conversation of compensation. I realize that not all projects can afford to hire folks, and that's an individual hurdle that each project will need to tackle, and is not something that I can offer any guidance on. You may hop on the Asset store, and see a pack like this and think to yourself, great this shouldn't be too expensive, but please remember that you aren't getting custom work that you sought out from a human who needs to eat and keep the lights on.

Negotiations and Payment

At the end of the day, that's up to you and your artist, but as a project manager you need to go into this contract with some preparation. I have been working in tech since the mid 2000's, and as of 2015 or so in Texas entry level tech jobs answering phones for custom products pay around 12-15$/hr. You can use something like glassdoor to see what skilled technical labor in the artists region pays. If you are seeking skilled labor, please do this first, so you don't undercut people, unless that's what you want to do. I also spoke to some people in the field, about what I was looking for and a ballpark on what I should pay.

After viewing the artists portfolios and armed with this information, I went into the conversation with my chosen artist and explained what I was looking for. This consisted of several things

  • Physical Description
    • A high fidelity 3D capital ship model for use in a video game
    • Turrets (Main turrets, and smaller Point Defense turrets)
    • Missile Silos
    • Munitions for the Armaments
    • Shapes (this matters depending on how realistic you are going. for example certain EM frequencies like Radar bounce off rounded surfaces easily) for aesthetics.
    • Any example work you would like elements drawn from
  • Spiritual Description
    • synopsis of the environment
    • synopsis of the government and type of crew
    • synopsis of technobabble (Newtonian movement physics? Do you need forward and rear facing engines for stopping without turning around, etc)

We then spent some time discussing fidelity levels, Turret design (requirements for game engine, how many articulating parts, etc), and how long the artist believe that it would take to do the work. I should also note that during this part of the conversation the artist brought up to me that unless I know exactly what I wanted that we should do a couple of rough outlines to pick a style.

We ended up agreeing on a set of 3D models for a flat price. Remember to speak with your artist about licensing.

  • 3 prototype ship designs
    • pick one of the 3 for the final style
  • 1 Hero Ship
  • Turrets
    • RailGun
    • Flak / Cannon
    • Missile
